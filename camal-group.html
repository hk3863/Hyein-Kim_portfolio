01

Overview

During my internship at CAMAL Group, I worked at the intersection of editorial operations and strategy. The work was not only about producing content. It was about structuring workflow, aligning contributors, and building a clearer content direction around business goals.

Two parts became especially important: coordinating a team of interns through a shared planning system, and developing a search strategy that could support the company's expansion into the Australian market.

02

Workflow coordination

As team lead for a group of three content interns, I managed the editorial workflow through a shared task system. I planned weekly cycles, assigned responsibilities, tracked progress, and made sure blogs and pillar pages were completed, reviewed, and finalized on schedule.

That experience pushed me beyond individual execution. It strengthened how I think about operational clarity, communication, and deadline management, especially when multiple contributors are working toward one publishing rhythm.

03

Australia strategy

One major goal during the internship was helping CAMAL Group grow its presence in the Australian B2B market. To support that shift, I developed a targeted SEO strategy grounded in Australian search behavior, competitor positioning, and local industry language within mining and procurement.

Instead of treating SEO as generic optimization, I used it as a strategic framing tool: what topics should the company own, which search intents mattered most, and how could each piece of content speak more directly to a regional audience.

04

SEO system

I conducted keyword research using Google Keyword Planner, mapped keyword clusters to blog topics and content pillars, and identified high-intent search terms specific to the Australian market. I also analyzed weekly Google Search Console data such as impressions, CTR, and top-performing queries to refine the strategy over time.

Alongside that, I created ChatGPT prompt templates for technical SEO blog writing so the team could apply heading hierarchy, meta description logic, slug structure, and internal linking more consistently across drafts.

05

Writing and optimization

I wrote and optimized industry-focused blog content, including mining conference guides, while applying technical SEO principles directly in the structure of each piece. That meant building clean heading systems, writing search-aware metadata, and making sure the content matched the vocabulary and information patterns users were already searching for.

The work helped connect content planning to business intent: each article needed to be useful editorially, discoverable in search, and aligned with the market the company wanted to grow into.

06

Reflection

This internship made strategy feel concrete to me. I was not only helping produce content. I was shaping systems for how work gets organized, how decisions get prioritized, and how a company can speak more clearly to a new audience.

It taught me that strategy often lives in the structure behind the output: the planning sheet, the keyword map, the editorial sequence, and the small operational decisions that let a team move with more clarity.
