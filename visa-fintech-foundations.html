Visa Fintech Foundations

A first-place capstone project proposing a new global monetary framework built around speed, trust, and interoperability.

01

Overview

Visa Fintech Foundations was a multi-week program focused on the future of money, banking systems, blockchain, and global payments. The capstone asked each team to analyze the failures of today's financial systems and design a more resilient global monetary framework.

Our team developed a concept called COWRY: a layered monetary system that uses existing settlement rails, improves speed with Layer 2 infrastructure, and adds a third coordination layer to make the system more transparent, inclusive, and stable.

02

Challenge

The core problem was not just currency. It was architecture. Cross-border payments remain slow and expensive, more than a billion people are still excluded from formal systems, trust in institutions is fragile, and legacy banking infrastructure was never designed for a hyper-connected global economy.

We used those failures as the basis for the narrative: money does not need a full replacement, but it does need an upgraded structure that can balance legality, speed, and trust at the same time.

03

Framework

The proposal was built as a three-layer system. Layer 1 holds secure settlement through central banks, CBDCs, stablecoins, and core blockchain infrastructure. Layer 2 acts as the fast lane: scalable, low-cost rails for remittances, micro-payments, and global commerce.

That already improved efficiency, but our main contribution was arguing that speed and settlement alone do not solve inequality or broken trust. The missing piece was a coordination layer.

04

Layer 3

We introduced Layer 3 as the trust and coordination layer. It included a global trust graph for early risk signals across institutions and networks, an inclusion engine for light-KYC and broader access, and fairness logic that could lower remittance fees, prioritize essential payments, and support crisis control.

We positioned Visa as the organization capable of connecting these layers: not by replacing the system, but by becoming the interoperability hub, trust operator, and standards layer for global money.

05

My role

My work focused on shaping how the concept was communicated. I helped frame Visa's role in the system, clarified the final vision and impact, and supported the presentation so the framework felt coherent, realistic, and persuasive rather than overly abstract.

What I enjoyed most in this project was turning a dense, systems-heavy idea into a story people could actually follow. It was a reminder that strategy is not only about the model itself, but about whether the audience can understand why it matters.

06

Outcome

Our team won 1st place in the capstone. More importantly, the project helped me practice something I care about deeply: synthesizing technical, systemic, and social issues into a clear strategic narrative.

The strongest part of this project was realizing that the future of money could be framed not as one new product, but as a better architecture.
